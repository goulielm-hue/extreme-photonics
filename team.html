The People Behind the Photons

Over two decades, this work has been carried by remarkable individuals — doctoral researchers, postdocs, visiting scientists, students, and collaborators — who passed through the lab and left their imprint on the science. Scroll down, and the present gives way to memory.

Rostock
2018 — present

The Extreme Photonics Labs at the University of Rostock, where the group builds the next generation of light-field synthesizers, pushes dynamic picoscopy toward valence-electron imaging, and explores quantum phase transitions at petahertz frequencies.

EG
Eleftherios Goulielmakis
Professor of Physics (W3) · Group Leader
Founded the Extreme Photonics Labs in 2018. ERC Advanced Grant holder. Pioneered light-field synthesis, attosecond optical pulses, and laser picoscopy of crystals.
AM
Annika Machotzek
Administrative Assistant
The indispensable backbone of the lab — coordination, administration, and the person who actually makes everything run.
SM
Dr. Santanu Mandal
Postdoctoral Researcher
Advancing laser-based microscopy with picometer resolution via high harmonic generation, studying extreme nonlinear behaviour of solids under intense light fields, and probing correlated materials at ultrafast time scales.
ZP
Zekun Pi
PhD Student
Development of new-generation ultrafast lasers and their characterisation. These lasers find applications in studying electrons on their native time scales.
PK

Before Rostock, there was Munich…

Munich & Garching
2005 — 2018

The Max Planck Institute of Quantum Optics and the Centre of Attosecond Science (MPC-AS) — where light-field synthesis was born, the first synthesized light transients were created, and the first real-time movie of valence electron motion was captured. A decade of breakthrough attosecond science.

AW
Adrian Wirth
PhD Student (2011)
Co-architect of synthesized light transients. Key contributor to the Science 2011 milestone on light-field synthesis.
MH
Mohammed Th. Hassan
PhD Student (2013)
Optical attosecond pulses and probing bound electrons. First to synthesize an optical attosecond pulse (Nature 2016).
Now Associate Professor, U. of Arizona
TL
Tran Trung Luu
PhD Student (2015)
Extreme UV high harmonic spectroscopy of solids (Nature 2015). Opened attosecond science to condensed matter.
Now Assistant Professor, HKU
AM
Antoine Moulet
PhD Student & Postdoc (2017)
Attosecond XUV-optical spectroscopy. First to probe soft x-ray excitons on attosecond timescales (Science 2017).
MG
Manish Garg
PhD Student (2017)

Now Senior Researcher, MPI Stuttgart
HL
Harshit Lakhotia

OR

…into the years where it all began.

Vienna & Crete
1999 — 2005

The formative years — first at FORTH in Heraklion, building high-order harmonic sources and learning to tame ultrashort pulses, then in Vienna and Munich where the first direct measurements of light waves led to a new era in attosecond physics.

DC
Dimitris Charalambidis
Supervisor at FORTH (Crete)
Guided early experiments in high harmonic generation and ultrafast interferometry at the Institute of Electronic Structure & Lasers.
NP
Nikos A. Papadogiannis
Collaborator (Crete)
Temporal characterization of harmonics, transmission grating interferometry for attosecond pulse characterization.
GN
Georgios Nersisyan
Collaborator (Crete)
Dispersionless Michelson interferometer for attosecond pulse characterization. Early HHG source development.
AB
Andrius Baltuška
Collaborator (Vienna / TU Wien)
Phase-controlled amplification of few-cycle pulses, carrier-envelope phase stabilization — essential technology for the attosecond revolution.
RK
Reinhard Kienberger
Collaborator (Vienna & MPQ)
Atomic Transient Recorder (Nature 2004), attosecond metrology with controlled light waveforms. Pioneering streaking measurements.
MU
Matthias Uiberacker
Collaborator (Vienna & MPQ)
Attosecond metrology with controlled light waveforms, direct measurement of light waves.
