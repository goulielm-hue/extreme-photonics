The shortest electron pulse ever created

In 2023, the generation of 53-attosecond electron pulses from a metallic nanotip was covered across the scientific press — from Nature to Physics World, Interesting Engineering, and media in Greece, Brazil, and Korea.

January 2023 · Nature 613, 662
Nature · EurekAlert · Physics Today

Laser takes pictures of electrons in crystals

The first real-space images of valence electrons in solids. Michael Sentef wrote in Nature that the work opens entirely new possibilities for understanding chemical bonding. Covered by AAAS, Physics Today, and press in Germany, Greece, and Brazil.

July 2020 · Nature 583, 55
Guinness World Records · Nature · New Scientist

Shortest pulse of visible light: 380 attoseconds

The optical attosecond pulse entered the Guinness Book of World Records and was named in Laser Focus World's top 20 photonics breakthroughs of 2016. Two Nature News & Views, New Scientist, Physics Today, and media worldwide.

2016 · Nature 530, 66 & Nature 538, 359
The Economist · Nature · New Scientist

Watching electrons move in real time

The Economist profiled the work as a landmark in physics. Olga Smirnova wrote in Nature about the implications. The first direct measurement of light waves in 2004 had already made headlines in Laser Focus World and OE Magazine — launching two decades of press coverage.

2004 – 2010 · Science 305, 1267 & Nature 466, 739
